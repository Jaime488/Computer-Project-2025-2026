K2NO

The K2NO is a customized Norwegian variant of South Korea’s K2 Black Panther, tailored for Norway’s Arctic terrain and operational needs under a 2022 contract worth $3.7 billion. It retains the core 120mm/L55 smoothbore gun and autoloader but adds Arctic-grade cold-weather systems, including engine preheaters and thermal insulation for extreme temperatures as low as -40°C. Enhanced protection features Norwegian-developed modular armor and the Trophy APS (instead of the Korean APS) to counter anti-tank missiles, alongside upgraded mine-resistant underbelly plating. Mobility is optimized with wider tracks for snow/soft terrain and a 1,500-hp MTU engine modified for Arctic reliability, though top speed remains 70 km/h. Deliveries begin in 2026, replacing Norway’s aging Leopard 2A4s and marking NATO’s first Arctic-specialized K2 variant.
