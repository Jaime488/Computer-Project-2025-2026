Altay (Prototype)

The Altay Prototype is Turkey's first indigenous third-generation main battle tank, developed by BMC under the National Tank Project to replace aging M48/M60 models, with its first prototype unveiled in 2011. Named after Ottoman General Fahrettin Altay, it features a 120mm/L55 smoothbore gun (license-produced Rheinmetall design) with an autoloader, advanced fire-control systems, and compatibility with LAHAT laser-guided missiles. Protection includes composite modular armor, pulverized armor (against HEAT rounds), and an optional AKKOR active protection system, while its 1,500-hp MTU Friedrichshafen diesel engine (planned for Turkish replacement) delivers a top speed of 70 km/h. Despite its promising design—including AI-assisted situational awareness and a crew of three (commander, gunner, driver)—the Altay faced delays due to engine embargoes (from Germany) and funding issues, pushing serial production to the late 2020s. The project remains a cornerstone of Turkey’s defense industry, with plans for Altay T1 (initial batch) and future T2/T3 variants featuring domestic powerplants and hybrid-electric drives.
