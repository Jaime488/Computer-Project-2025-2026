T-84 Oplot

The T-84 Oplot is Ukraine's most advanced main battle tank, developed in the 1990s as an evolution of the Soviet-era T-80UD with substantial upgrades in firepower, protection, and mobility. It features a 125mm KBA-3 smoothbore gun capable of firing APFSDS, HEAT, and guided missiles, paired with a modern fire-control system including thermal imaging for superior accuracy day or night. The tank's defenses include Kontakt-5 explosive reactive armor (ERA) and the optional Duplet ERA system, along with a Shtora-1 electro-optical jammer to disrupt enemy targeting. Powered by a 1,200-horsepower 6TD-2 diesel engine, the T-84 achieves a top speed of 70 km/h and excels in maneuverability despite its 48-ton weight. While highly capable, the T-84 has seen limited production due to Ukraine's defense budget constraints, with most units serving in the Ukrainian Army and a small number exported to Thailand.
